Born in 1940, he founded Grameen Bank in 1983, revolutionizing poverty alleviation through microcredit—small loans to empower the poor, particularly women, to start businesses and achieve financial independence. His innovative approach transformed lives worldwide, inspiring global microfinance initiatives. Despite facing criticism and political challenges, his work remains influential, earning him numerous awards and recognition as a pioneer in sustainable development and financial inclusion for marginalized communities.
